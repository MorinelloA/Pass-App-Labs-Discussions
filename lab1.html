Let's Shape the Future of Pass App! Help us design haptic feedback (vibrations) that make your wallet interactions feel magical. Earn Pass Perks for your ideas! --- What is Haptic Feedback? Haptic feedback is subtle vibrations that apps use to make actions feel tangible. (Example: Your phone vibrates slightly when you send a message or confirm a transaction.) Why It Matters for Pass App? As a web3 wallet, we want to use haptics to: ✅ Celebrate successes (e.g., transaction confirmed!) ✅ Warn about risks (e.g., high gas fees) ✅ Make interactions feel smooth and satisfying. --- Your Mission (Two Parts): 1️⃣ Share "Delight Moments" Where should Pass App add haptic feedback to make your experience more rewarding?
  • Example: A gentle pulse when a transaction succeeds.
  • Example: A short vibration when you unlock your wallet securely.
2️⃣ Scout Other Apps This week, pay attention to apps you use daily:
  • Where do they use haptic feedback well?
  • What feels annoying or unnecessary?
Example: "Signal app vibrates when a message sends — feels satisfying!" "My banking app vibrates too much during login — it's distracting." --- How to Participate 1️⃣ Post your "delight moment" ideas for Pass App in this channel 2️⃣ Give examples of haptics you love/hate in other apps (Feel free to post screenshots) 3️⃣ Respond to the feedback others (Disagreement is good! But always be respectful!!) 4️⃣ Earn Pass Perks! ️ This runs from now until Tuesday, March 4th Let's create a wallet that feels as good as it works! (edited)

TAE_86 2/27/2025 7:37 PM
I only started using Pass very recently, but i'll do my best to leave some ideas here:
  • Outgoing txs: When a successful transaction goes out, a short vibration could be done to show that it was successful. Likewise, if there was an issue, a longer, stronger vibration could be done to indicate an issue that needs addressed
  • Incoming tx: I would assume that 99% of incoming transactions will be done when the wallet is closed (and the user will be alerted via a push notification). However, maybe theres a welcome screen of some type that shows an overview of all transactions since the last login whenever the user open the app? This could vibrate as well.
  • Fee adjustments: If the user is in the middle of making a transaction or swap, and is taking a long time, the app could vibrate to try and push them along. This could correspond to whenever the tx fee is close to resetting (assuming this app has that)
  • Field Verification: Whenever the user enters an invalid value into any field (such a entering a BTC address when an ETH address is required), a strong pulse could alert the user of the mistake These are just a few ideas. I'll think about it as I explore the app some more

I hope my feedback isn't coming in too late. I think the beauty in haptics is in how subtle they can be. How they can take effect without you even noticing them. Shorter taps for a successful transaction. Light taps for switches. Just suggesting though.
